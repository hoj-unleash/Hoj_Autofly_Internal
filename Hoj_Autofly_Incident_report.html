Working Assumptions

Assumption
Geofence missions may be the specific trigger
Multiple uncommanded movement incidents appear to occur on or shortly after geofence missions, at low altitude just after takeoff. This is a relatively new mission type for Endeavour and introduces new Autofly-driven automation compared to manual flights.
Assumption
RTK behaviour may be a contributing factor
Steve Catto observed that swapping two crews to Mondopin RTK appeared to stop the uncommanded movement problem for those teams. The 12 Feb crash AFL log shows RTK dropping mid-flight, though causality is disputed.
Assumption
12 Feb crash has conflicting causation signals
The CSV shows extreme stick deflection; the AFL log shows RTK loss at the same time; the pilot denies any inputs. All three readings directly contradict each other. .DAT files are required to resolve this.
Assumption
Downlink loss is a hardware / RF issue
Kurtis's downlink pattern ‚Äî same aircraft and RC on all three dates, worsening ‚Äî points to a hardware-specific fault. RC signal reads 100% during dropouts, consistent with data packet loss rather than RF carrier loss. Unrelated to Autofly.
